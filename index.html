What is VRP?

An exec summary by Stateless Group, the consultancy who led Open Banking's delivery of the world's first VRP Standard.

Variable Recurring Payments (VRPs) are a new addition to Open Banking that allow a customer to safely connect services to their bank account so that those services can make instant bank transfers automatically on their behalf.

VRPs are an alternative method to card payments that are safer and more efficient. They enable innovation in payment UX, reduction in processing fees, better control of risk, faster settlement, and the creation of new types of services for customers involving the intelligent programmatic movement of their money.

Some examples of where VRP will improve over card payments:

  • Automatic bill payments
  • “One click” payments
  • Frictionless payments at the point of sale
  • Automated savings and financial management (“Sweeping”)

How does VRP work?

Put simply, VRP entails a customer leaving permission (“Consent”) with their bank for a third party service to be able to make bank transfers, in the background, on their behalf.

There are 5 key steps to the VRP process:

Step 1 - Agreeing consent

Setting up a VRP begins by the customer agreeing with the service provider under what conditions they agree for payments to be made on their behalf. Those conditions restrict things like the destination accounts they can send to, the amounts they are able to move, the frequency with which payments can be made, the date the permission expires, etc. In VRP, we call those conditions “Consent Parameters”.

Step 2 - Redirection to bank app

Once agreed, the service provider refers the customer to their bank app with this consent information, so that the bank can know under what conditions they should honour payment instructions from the service provider for that customer.

Step 3 - Strong Customer Authentication with bank

The bank displays the details of the consent to the customer and securely authenticates them, confirming the customer agrees. The bank records the agreed consent and creates an associated access token for the service provider. The bank then refers the customer back to the service provider with that access token.

Step 4 - Redirection back to service provider app

The customer delivers the access token to the service provider, which is then stored against the customer’s account for later use. At this point, the “VRP consent setup” has been completed.

Step 5 - Ongoing VRP Payments

From now on, the service provider can use the access token to instruct transfers from the customer’s account on their behalf (“VRP Payments”), provided the usage is within the conditions of the agreed consent parameters. If the service provider attempts to make a payment that would violate those conditions in any way, the bank will reject the service provider’s instruction.
